The reason I like and encourage Linux

It is no secret that I hate Windows and Mac OS for various reasons. However the reason I tend to encourage and help folks try Linux is not primarily due to my hatred of Microsoft and Apple(it certainly is a strong motivator though lol), but rather I found that it became a very empowering and confidence building experience for myself and people I helped get set up. It can and will be frustrating at times, but when you work through it's an awesome feeling of gaining more understanding how things work and more control over your system(s).

That control and confidence goes a very long way toward having in my opinion a more positive outlook on computers overall and from what I've experienced at least...it opened up more options than what I and others lost leaving Windows and Mac as I became better at using Linux. That growth mixed with the continued development of Linux packages(applications /software) by various communities has admittedly been a driver for me opening up more to people again too.

I also feel that in turn it gives a pretty good sense of community. The Linux community has their fair share of toxic elements much like any other, but when you get past those knuckleheads you see A LOT of really helpful, open, and community oriented folks who try to contribute jn their own little way. Whether it's a developer, a distro maintainer, a user giving feedback, or someone giving tech help it all..."fits" and that's pretty cool to see. It's kinda like Cohost in some ways where the more the community puts in the more the community gets out. So I try to do my little share as well and help people who are interested in trying and or using Linux more...

I don't know...this all came up after getting a thank you from a couple people recently I helped setup awhile ago and they seem to really run with it and doing some stuff I wasn't even aware of. I'm rambling maybe, but I just think that's cool.

EDIT For clarity this is not me saying people should outright abandon their current setups. I am aware and understand people for various reasons can't daily drive Linux for their needs at a given time and that is entirely valid. My job currently uses Adobe for very specific tasks that can't be done in any other software without a huge overhead for instance so we keep a windows and MacOS machine around for those things. This is primarily to highlight reasons I like using and helping people who are interested in trying and using Linux that isn't just "Windows bad!" Or "just use Linux", neither of which I do. Rather I encourage and support people who want to take that on.
