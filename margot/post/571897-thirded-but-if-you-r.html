seconding this (although imo you can give to Wikipedia if you want to [edit much later: those in the comments and @margot were very convincing and yeah don't donate to Wikipedia ha]); not only is the Internet Archive saving sites, it's also saving the last copies of legally-recorded decommoditized entertainment-- broadcast television. I've watched the entirety of the X-Files along with several other TV shows that were on when broadcast television was big and free

the Internet Archive also offers free file storage and distribution to anyone. that's right-- if you're too broke or non-technical to rent or build your own file distribution solution, the Internet Archive has you covered, and they are not turning you into a product to do this (like Google does).

if you're a video artist, take a look at the thousands of hours of footage from the Prelinger Archives, which you can help sort. there's home movies and industrial films and educational trash for American classrooms

if you're a musician or lover of music, check out the Live Music Archive. yeah yeah there's a shitload of Dead and jam bands in there, sure, what did you expect but also! check out what other bands have thriving tape-exchanges on IA:

  • Smashing Pumpkins
  • Ween
  • Meat Puppets
  • Godspeed You! Black Emperor
  • Mountain Goats
  • Low
  • Fugazi
  • Belle and Sebastian

... and literally much much more

IA rules, everyone should be using it
